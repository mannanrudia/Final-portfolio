The Problem

Trust is Dead in
Food Delivery.

Rapido was entering food delivery with a radical promise: zero commission for restaurants, no hidden charges for consumers. On paper, a game-changer. In reality? Nobody believed it.

"Indian consumers have been burned by Swiggy surge pricing and Zomato platform fees too many times to trust the word 'free' again."

The challenge wasn't product — it was trust. How do you make radical transparency your competitive moat, not just a tagline?

🔍
The Strategy

Make Transparency
the Campaign.

Every competitor was hiding something. Rapido's only advantage was having nothing to hide. So we turned hiding nothing into the entire creative platform.

🪟

Glass Box Identity

"Nothing to Hide" built into the visual DNA. Glass boxes, clear pricing displays, see-through brand experiences. Make it literal.

📋

HIDDEN COSTS: 0

Every piece of content led with the number zero where competitors show a fee. The absence of a charge as the hero feature.

📱

Challenger Playbook

Week-by-week content calendar calling out the industry's hidden fee culture — and positioning Ownly as the honest alternative.

The Execution

Content That
Built Belief.

01

Invite Ownly Campaign

Referral-first launch — only invite friends to get access. Creates scarcity, drives organic word-of-mouth, makes early adopters feel like insiders.

02

Glass Box Activations

Physical "glass box" brand experiences where consumers could literally see the pricing structure. No secrets. Theatre of transparency.

03

Pink Visibility Drive

Rapido + Ownly uniforms in bold pink — high-visibility in public spaces. Every Rapido driver became a walking billboard for the launch.

"We Play Fair" claw machine — no rigged mechanics, transparent rules, every customer wins. A physical metaphor for the entire brand ethos.
